About This Program

Discover Who You Are in Christ

Jr. High Week is designed for students navigating the critical transition from childhood to adolescence, where questions of identity, belonging, and faith become central to their development.

At Bear Lake Camp, we create a safe, Christ-centered environment where 6th-8th graders can explore their identity in Christ through engaging Bible teaching, authentic worship, and meaningful relationships. Our experienced counselors and speakers understand the unique challenges of early adolescence and help campers build a foundation of faith that will sustain them through high school and beyond.

Each day includes interactive Bible studies, outdoor adventures, camp-wide games, and evening worship gatherings designed to challenge students spiritually while giving them space to have fun and build lifelong friendships.

What to Bring

Jr. High Packing List

Clothing

▼
  • 6-7 t-shirts (camp gets messy!)
  • 2-3 pairs of shorts
  • 1 pair of long pants (for cooler evenings)
  • 1 sweatshirt or jacket
  • Underwear and socks for each day
  • Modest swimsuit (one-piece or tankini for girls)
  • Pajamas
  • Comfortable walking shoes/sneakers
  • Flip-flops or sandals for shower

Bedding & Toiletries

▼
  • Sleeping bag or twin sheets and blanket
  • Pillow
  • Towel and washcloth
  • Toothbrush and toothpaste
  • Shampoo and soap
  • Deodorant
  • Sunscreen (SPF 30+ recommended)
  • Bug spray
  • Any prescription medications (give to nurse on arrival)

Other Essentials

▼
  • Bible, notebook, and pen
  • Water bottle (refill stations throughout camp)
  • Flashlight or headlamp
  • Backpack or day bag
  • Sunglasses and hat
  • Laundry bag for dirty clothes
  • Rain jacket (weather can be unpredictable)

Optional Items

▼
  • Camera (disposable or waterproof)
  • Book for free time
  • Modest costume/outfit for theme night
  • Stamps and stationery (for parent letters)

Please DO NOT Bring

▼
  • Cell phones, tablets, gaming devices (we provide a phone for emergencies)
  • Expensive jewelry or electronics
  • Fireworks, lighters, or matches
  • Weapons of any kind (including pocket knives)
  • Inappropriate clothing (see dress code in FAQ)
  • Pets

Lost & Found: We do our best to return lost items, but please label everything with your camper's name. Unclaimed items are donated 30 days after camp ends.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What if my child has dietary restrictions or food allergies?

▼

We accommodate most dietary needs including vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, and dairy-free diets. During registration, you'll indicate any allergies or restrictions, and our kitchen staff will prepare alternative meals as needed. For severe allergies (e.g., nut allergies), our camp nurse will review your camper's emergency action plan upon arrival.

Is there cell phone access? Can my child call home?

▼

We are a "device-free" camp to help students fully engage with their peers and God without distractions. Cell phones are collected on arrival and returned at pickup. If your camper needs to contact you (or vice versa), our office phone is available during designated times. In case of emergency, our staff will contact you immediately.

What is the counselor-to-camper ratio?

▼

For Jr. High Week, we maintain a 1:6 counselor-to-camper ratio. Each cabin has 2 counselors for approximately 12 campers, ensuring personalized attention and safety. All counselors are background-checked, trained in first aid/CPR, and selected for their spiritual maturity and heart for students.

What does a typical day look like at Jr. High camp?

▼

Sample Daily Schedule:

  • 7:30 AM - Wake up and cabin cleanup
  • 8:00 AM - Breakfast
  • 9:00 AM - Morning worship and teaching
  • 10:30 AM - Small group discussion
  • 12:00 PM - Lunch
  • 1:00 PM - Free time (lake, gym, games)
  • 3:00 PM - Afternoon activities (high ropes, crafts, sports)
  • 5:30 PM - Dinner
  • 7:00 PM - Evening worship and testimony time
  • 9:00 PM - Cabin time (games, devotions, prayer)
  • 10:30 PM - Lights out

What is your dress code policy?

▼

We ask campers to dress modestly out of respect for themselves and others. Guidelines include:

  • Shorts and skirts should be fingertip length or longer
  • Tank tops should have 2-inch wide straps (no spaghetti straps)
  • Shirts should cover midriff when arms are raised
  • Swimsuits: one-piece or modest tankini for girls; board shorts for guys
  • No clothing with inappropriate language, images, or references to drugs/alcohol

If a camper brings clothing that doesn't meet these guidelines, we'll ask them to change or provide camp-appropriate alternatives.

What if my child has never been to camp before or struggles with homesickness?

▼

Homesickness is completely normal, especially for first-time campers! Our counselors are trained to recognize and address homesickness with compassion. Strategies include:

  • Keeping campers engaged in activities
  • Buddy system to build quick friendships
  • One-on-one check-ins from counselors
  • Encouragement to focus on the present moment rather than counting down days

If homesickness persists and impacts your child's ability to participate, we'll contact you to discuss next steps. However, most campers overcome homesickness within the first 24-48 hours!
