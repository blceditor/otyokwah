Straight Talk

Is This Camp Right for Your Kid?

Here's the deal:

This isn't your typical "church camp" where kids sit through sermons all day. It's a place where middle schoolers can explore faith on their own terms—no pressure, just honest conversations about the big questions they're already asking.

Your kid will fit in here if they: Are curious (or skeptical) about faith, want to make real friends, need a break from screens, or just want to try something new. No faith background required. Questions and doubts? Welcome.

You'll feel good about sending them if: You want trained, background-checked counselors (1:6 ratio), a community that values mental health and inclusivity, and an experience that's more about discovering who they are than being told who to be.

About This Week

A Place Where Faith Gets Real

Jr. High Week is for students navigating the messy, exciting transition from kid to teenager— where figuring out who you actually are becomes the main quest.

At Bear Lake Camp, we create a safe space where 6th-8th graders can explore faith, build deep friendships, and discover who they're meant to be. No fake answers. No judgment. Just real conversations with people who actually get what it's like to be your age.

Our counselors aren't here to preach at you—they're here to walk alongside you. Each day includes exploring big questions together, outdoor adventures that push you out of your comfort zone, camp-wide games that bring out your competitive side, and evening gatherings where we create space for you to connect with something bigger than yourself.

Common Questions

Real Questions from Real Parents (and Campers)

No sugarcoating. Here are the honest answers to what people actually want to know.

Is this camp just for super religious kids?

▼

Nope. We get campers from all kinds of backgrounds—some grew up in church, some have never stepped foot in one, some are questioning everything they were taught. All are welcome. You don't need to have it all figured out. You don't even need to be sure you believe. We're here to create a safe space to explore, not to pressure you into anything.

What if my kid isn't sure what they believe?

▼

Perfect. That's exactly the kind of honest place we want campers to start from. Our counselors are trained to meet kids where they are—not where we think they should be. Doubt isn't a problem to fix, it's part of the journey. We create space for questions, disagreement, and the messy process of figuring out what you actually believe.

Will my kid be forced to pray/worship if they're uncomfortable?

▼

No pressure, just invitation. We gather as a community for music and reflection, but participation is always an invitation, never a demand. If your kid wants to sit quietly, journal, or just observe—totally fine. Our goal is to create a space where they can connect with something bigger than themselves, however that looks for them. Forced faith isn't real faith.

What's the deal with the no-phone policy? Why is it so strict?

▼

We know it's hard. Here's why we do it: In our years running camp, we've seen that real friendships and honest conversations happen when screens aren't in the way. It's not about punishment—it's about giving your kid a rare gift: a week to be fully present.

Phones are collected on arrival and returned at pickup. If your camper needs to contact you (or you need to reach them), our office phone is available during designated times. In emergencies, we'll contact you immediately. Most parents tell us their kid was resistant at first but ended up grateful for the break.

How do you handle bullying or exclusion?

▼

Zero tolerance. Full stop. We actively teach and model inclusion from day one. Our counselors are trained to spot exclusion early and intervene immediately. If bullying occurs, we address it directly with the campers involved, contact parents, and in serious cases, may send a camper home.

We also build community intentionally—mixing up groups for activities, rotating table seating, and creating opportunities for campers to connect across cabin lines. Our goal is for every kid to feel like they belong, exactly as they are.

What if my child has dietary restrictions or food allergies?

▼

We accommodate most dietary needs including vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, and dairy-free. During registration, you'll indicate any allergies or restrictions, and our kitchen staff will prepare alternative meals. For severe allergies (e.g., nut allergies), our camp nurse will review your camper's emergency action plan on arrival.

What is the counselor-to-camper ratio?

▼

1:6 counselor-to-camper ratio. Each cabin has 2 counselors for approximately 12 campers, ensuring personalized attention and safety. All counselors are background-checked, trained in first aid/CPR, and chosen for their ability to connect with middle schoolers and create a safe, inclusive environment.

What is your dress code policy?

▼

We ask campers to dress modestly out of respect for themselves and others. Guidelines:

  • Shorts and skirts should be fingertip length or longer
  • Tank tops should have 2-inch wide straps (no spaghetti straps)
  • Shirts should cover midriff when arms are raised
  • Swimsuits: one-piece or modest tankini for girls; board shorts for guys
  • No clothing with inappropriate language, images, or references to drugs/alcohol

If a camper brings clothing that doesn't meet guidelines, we'll ask them to change or provide camp-appropriate alternatives. No shaming, just a gentle redirect.

What if my kid has never been to camp or struggles with homesickness?

▼

Homesickness is totally normal, especially for first-time campers. Our counselors are trained to spot it early and respond with compassion. Strategies include:

  • Keeping campers engaged in activities (distraction helps)
  • Buddy system to build quick friendships
  • One-on-one check-ins from counselors
  • Encouraging focus on the present moment (not counting down days)

Most campers overcome homesickness within the first 24-48 hours once they've made friends and settled into the rhythm. If it persists and impacts their ability to participate, we'll contact you to discuss next steps. But honestly? By day 3, most kids don't want to leave.
